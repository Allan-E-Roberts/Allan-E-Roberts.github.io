Reading Nook

The scent of ink and paper
  Cluttered shelves line the walls

Stacks of books upon a desk
  A sofa too big and too small

A ray of gold pierces through
  The dusty window pane

It lights the floor and timbers
  Reveals the redwood veins

A picture on the shelf
  Another just behind

Tell a tale of travelers
  Or time left behind

Come in and rest a while
  Go ahead and take a look

But leave a new story behind
  Within the reading nook

Rainy Cabin

Warm your hands before the flames
  that flicker in the night

The rain that drips and splatters
  will bring the fields to life

Now close your eyes, snuggle close
  night won’t last forever

We’re wrapped up tight, warm inside
  nothing could be better

Snowstorm

I rest my tired hands
  Take off my frozen coat

Remove my soaking socks
  Place my boots against the stove

Some fear the winter wind
  Call it a frozen hell

But I hold a cup of cocoa
  Within my house I dwell

The wind, it blows, it howls
  Within the darkest night

I sit upon a sofa
  The fire’s only light

A scent of simmering soup
  Above the roaring flame

No matter where I go
  My home this will remain

Tanksgiving

As the leaves begin to change
  frost’s slowly setting in

Take a moment, take a breath
  look at the year again

Things you learned & said & done
  some hard but many fun

Look back to those whome you love
  both family & friends

Feast on the fruits of work you’ve done
  relax before the hearth

Tell tales & stories, silly jokes
  play games & raise a toast

Soon enough the time will come
  to return to work

Bur rest for now, remember then
  what really matters most

Christmast Snow


This winter night
        a cold delight
                alights upon my tongue

It once was there
        is now nowhere
                ‘tis gone & done yet fun

The Christmas lights
        shine bright tonight
                sparkle on some of them

I watch the sky
        reach up high
                and catch another one

Allan E. Roberts
